def subcategorybar(X, vals, width=0.8):
    n = len(vals)
    _X = np.arange(len(X))
    for i in range(n):
        plt.bar(_X - width/2. + i/float(n)*width, vals[i], 
                width=width/float(n), align="edge")   
    plt.xticks(_X, X)

4.1 Process data¶

I drop 'Neighborhood', 'full_location_name', 'HouseStyle' and 'SaleCondition' since these features need one hot encoding. But there are too many different tags of a feature, so performing one hot encoding for them may cause sparse matrix(too many columns in my dataset). As a result, I decide to drop them. Then I will do one hot encoding for 'LotShape':'Reg', 'IR1', 'IR2', 'IR3' to maek them suitable for training model. Fianlly, I will get my prediction formula which is the purpose of this tutorial.¶
